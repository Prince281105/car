Ker Sangri

Ingredients:

  • Dried Ker berries - 1/2 cup
  • Dried Sangri beans - 1/2 cup
  • Onions - 1 medium, chopped
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium, chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tbsp
  • Green chilies - 2-3, slit
  • Red chili powder - 1 tsp
  • Cumin seeds - 1/2 tsp
  • Turmeric powder - 1/4 tsp
  • Amchoor (dried mango powder) - 1 tbsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil (mustard oil or vegetable oil) - 2 tbsp
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish) - a handful

Method:

  1. Soak the Ker and Sangri: Soak the dried Ker berries and Sangri beans in warm water for about 30 minutes, or until they soften. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté the spices: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the spices: Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add the tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices.
  5. Add the Ker and Sangri: Add the soaked Ker berries and Sangri beans to the pan. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender.
  6. Add the amchoor: Add amchoor (dried mango powder) to the pan and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Season and serve: Season with salt. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with roti, rice, or paratha.
