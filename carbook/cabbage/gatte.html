Gatte ki Sabji

Ingredients:

  • Gram flour (besan) - 1 cup
  • Yogurt - 1 cup
  • Onions - 1 medium, chopped
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium, chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tbsp
  • Green chilies - 2-3, slit
  • Cumin seeds - 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder - 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Red chili powder - 1/2 tsp (optional)
  • Heeng (asafoetida) - a pinch
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil (mustard oil or vegetable oil) - 2 tbsp
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish) - a handful

Method:

  1. Prepare the gatte: In a bowl, combine gram flour, yogurt, salt, and a little water. Mix well to form a smooth dough. Add more water if needed.
  2. Shape the gatte: Shape the dough into small balls or dumplings (gatte) and set aside.
  3. Cook the gatte: In a pot, bring water to a boil. Add the gatte and cook for about 10-15 minutes, or until they are cooked through and float to the surface.
  4. Sauté the spices: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  5. Add the spices: Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder (optional), and heeng. Sauté for a minute.
  6. Add the tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices.
  7. Add the gatte: Add the cooked gatte to the pan. Mix well and simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until the flavors meld.
  8. Season and serve: Season with salt. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with roti, rice, or paratha.
